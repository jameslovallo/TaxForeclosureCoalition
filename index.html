You Can Help Stop Unconstitutional Property Tax Foreclosures in Detroit

From 2011 to 2015, the Wayne County treasurer foreclosed upon about 1 in 4 Detroit properties for nonpayment of property taxes. The Great Depression was the last time in American history that we experienced this record numbers of property tax foreclosures.

According to the Michigan Constitution, no property should be assessed at more than 50% of its market value. But, between 2009-2015, the City of Detroit assessed 55% to 85% of its properties in violation of its state constitution. Since property taxes were based on these ridiculous and illegally inflated numbers, it is no surprise that residents weren't able to pay. As a result, over 100,000 working families have lost their homes, and many Detroit neighborhoods have been devastated. African-Americans have been hit hardest of all.



The Coalition to Stop Unconstitutional Property Tax Foreclosures is fighting to win three demands:

  1. Stop unconstitutional property tax assessments.
  2. Compensate Detroit residents who have already lost their homes through illegal tax foreclosures.
    Learn More
  3. Suspend pending property tax foreclosures until we can confirm that delinquent taxpayers have not been unconstitutionally assessed.

Peaceful Protest Near Treasurer's Home to Save Other Family Homes
Call for foreclosure moratorium until illegal property tax assessments stop

A group of Detroit families working to save their homes will be joined by supporters outside the home of Wayne County Treasurer Eric Sabree in a peaceful call for the Treasurer to protect Detroit families from illegal tax foreclosure. The group will gather Thursday, August 31 at 11:00 a.m. at 17401 Pontchartrain in Detroit to call for a moratorium on kicking people out of their homes until the problem of illegal tax foreclosures can be solved. A recent study by Professors Atuahene and Hodge found that between 2009 and 2015, the City of Detroit assessed up to 85 percent of properties at values that violated the Michigan Constitution, which states that no property should be assessed at more than 50 percent of its market value. The study also found that higher-valued homes are being assessed significantly under 50% of their market value while, in a cruel irony, lower-valued homes are being assessed at over 50% of their market value.

As a result, poor and working class Detroit families could not afford the illegal property tax assessments and the resulting over-inflated property tax bills, and so Wayne County authorities foreclosed on their homes for non-payment of property taxes. Between 2011-2015, 1 in 4 Detroit properties have been taken from their owners in tax foreclosure proceedings. No American city has witnessed this exorbitantly high number of property tax foreclosures since the Great Depression.

Wayne County Treasurer Eric Sabree has the power to stop this by placing a moratorium on tax foreclosures until the problem of unconstitutional property tax assessments is remedied. But, he has so far refused to do the right thing. He is only offering payment plans to help Detroit residents pay taxes that were, in fact, illegally charged. That is no help at all.

Even worse, Mr. Sabree -- Detroit resident -- is one of the fortunate few Detroiters whose home value is relatively high and therefore systematically assessed at a lower value than the law allows. The Detroit assessor estimated that the market value of Mr. Sabree's home was only $180,000. But, an independent analysis suggests the value of Mr. Sabree's home and neighboring houses is upwards of $400,000.

Do You Want to See Unjust Property Tax Foreclosure Ended? Come and support the class action lawsuit brought by the ACLU & NAACP Legal Defense Fund.

In Morning v. Sabree, several Detroit homeowners and four neighborhood associations appealed to the Michigan Court of Appeals after the lower court dismissed their claim under the Federal Housing Act against Wayne County. The claim against Wayne County alleges that its Treasurer's practice of foreclosing properties - despite the well-known unconstitutional over-assessments underlying each tax debt - is illegal housing discrimination against African Americans. Because the lower court dismissed this claim against Wayne County, the appeal (which will be argued at the hearing) gives the legal team a chance to get that claim back in court and keep it alive.

Come to the hearing, and get educated about the lawsuit. Your presence will let the judges know that Detroit is woke and will no longer stand for unconstitutional property tax foreclosures.
